# 01 — Positioning & messaging

This is the source of truth for what the site argues and how it sounds. Every page inherits from here.

## The one-line positioning

**Kind Stranger is a community marketing agency.**

We help companies, organizations, and public figures earn a real place in the communities that matter to them — from high-level strategy to boots-on-the-ground execution, built alongside you. Reddit is our sharpest specialty; it is not our ceiling, and neither is any single kind of client.

## The category we're claiming

**Community marketing.** Not "Reddit marketing," not "social," not "content." The discipline is: earning genuine visibility and trust inside the communities where the people you're trying to reach already spend time and form opinions — and doing it as a real participant, not an intruder. "The room" might be a subreddit full of engineers, a niche Discord, a professional forum, a Slack community, or the comment sections that decide how a public figure is seen. Same discipline, different rooms.

Why this framing matters (context for the builder, not copy): "Reddit agency" makes Kind Stranger a *tactic* that's easy to commoditize and cap. "Community marketing agency" makes it a *discipline* that can sell strategy, multiple channels, and embedded leadership — with Reddit as the flagship proof that the team can do the hardest version of the job.

## The point of view (the argument the site makes)

People have quietly stopped trusting what organizations say about themselves. They trust peers, practitioners, and the communities they already belong to. So the highest-leverage marketing isn't shouting louder in ad units — it's showing up, usefully and honestly, in the rooms where your audience actually talks, compares notes, and decides. This holds whether you're a startup, a nonprofit, a consumer brand, or a person with a public profile.

Most companies get this wrong in one of two ways: they ignore communities entirely, or they barge in to pitch and get (rightly) torched. Kind Stranger does the third thing — the hard thing — which is to become a genuinely valued presence in those rooms. That's the whole idea behind the name.

## The brand idea: "kind stranger"

The name *is* the methodology. You earn attention in a community by being the generous, helpful stranger who adds something — not the marketer who crashes the party to sell. This is the thread that should run through the entire site: candor, usefulness, and respect for the room. It's also the anti-shill promise clients are quietly buying.

## Messaging pillars

Use these four as the backbone of the homepage and as reusable proof throughout.

**1. Community is the new front door.**
Trust has moved from ads to peers. Your next customers are forming opinions in communities right now — with or without you in the room.

**2. We're practitioners, not posters.**
We participate, we don't spam. We learn a community's norms, earn standing, and add value first. No astroturf, no drive-by pitches, no fake accounts. (This is the "kind stranger" ethos made concrete.)

**3. Reddit is where we go deepest.**
It's the hardest community channel to fake and the one we've mastered — which makes it the best proof that we can earn trust anywhere your buyers gather. It's a flagship, not the whole menu.

**4. Visibility that compounds.** *(the quiet second argument — see language rules below)*
The rooms where people ask questions are increasingly where the answers get written — and where they get repeated, quoted, and summarized long after. Show up credibly and the same work pays off twice: with the people asking, and with everything that repeats the answer back to them.

**5. Strategy to boots on the ground — built with you.**
We work across the whole spectrum: the high-level strategy *and* the day-to-day doing. We're not an agency that hands you a deck and disappears — we build it out alongside you, in the rooms, in the weeds, as an extension of your team.

## Language rules for pillar 4 (important)

Make the point; never name the mechanism. **Do not** write "AEO," "answer engine optimization," "generative engine optimization," "GEO," "LLM," "ChatGPT," "AI search," or "get cited by AI." Allude with phrasing like:

- "…where the answers get written — and repeated."
- "The internet increasingly answers questions by summarizing what communities already said. We help you be part of that answer."
- "Being genuinely useful in public compounds. People find it later; so does everything that repeats it."

A sharp CMO will read the subtext. Everyone else just reads it as "durable word-of-mouth." That's the intent.

## Voice & tone

Bridge from Barrie's own voice and the birthday site's candor into B2B. The birthday site said true things plainly ("Is Pura Vida good? Not really.") and treated the reader like a friend ("Talk to a Person"). Carry that over.

- **Confident, plainspoken, a little dry.** No consultant-speak, no "unlock synergies," no "leverage." If a sentence sounds like a SaaS landing page generator wrote it, cut it.
- **Honest to a fault.** Say what community marketing *isn't*. Name the way most people get it wrong. Admit Reddit will torch you if you fake it. Candor is the credibility.
- **Human, not corporate.** Short sentences. Real words. The occasional dry joke. Never cutesy, never hype.
- **Respectful of the reader's intelligence.** They're a B2B operator. Don't explain what Reddit is. Don't oversell.

Anti-patterns to avoid: exclamation-point energy, "revolutionary," "game-changing," emoji everywhere, walls of adjectives, fake urgency.

## Objection handling (weave into copy where natural; also good FAQ material)

- *"Isn't Reddit hostile to marketers?"* — Yes, to bad ones. That hostility is exactly why doing it right is a moat. We earn standing before we ever mention a client.
- *"Can you measure community marketing?"* — Yes: share of voice, qualified traffic, sentiment, branded search lift, and the conversations we can point to. We'll tell you what we can and can't attribute.
- *"Does this work for our kind of organization?"* — Almost certainly. B2B startups, consumer brands, nonprofits, public figures — if the people you care about gather somewhere and talk, community marketing works. Niche, high-trust rooms are where it works best, and B2B is where we've gone deepest.
- *"Can't we just do this ourselves?"* — You can. Most teams don't have the time to earn standing in a dozen communities without tripping the shill alarm. That's what we're for.

## Words we use / words we avoid

- **Use:** community marketing, earn, standing, participant, the room, show up, useful, honest, flagship, practitioners, receipts.
- **Avoid:** growth-hacking, engagement, synergy, leverage (verb), disrupt, ninja/guru, "content is king," and the entire AEO/AI-search vocabulary (see rules above).
