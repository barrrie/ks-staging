## /what-we-do — Community marketing overview

**Purpose:** Establish community marketing as the umbrella discipline, then lay out the service set beneath it. This is where the "we're a discipline, not a tactic" story fully lands.

**Sections:**

1. **Eyebrow** `WHAT WE DO` · **H1:** Community marketing, from strategy to standing.
   **Intro:** We help companies, organizations, and public figures show up where the people they care about actually make decisions — the communities, forums, and threads that polished marketing never reaches. Some of that is Reddit. A lot of it isn't. And we work across the whole range — from setting the strategy to putting boots on the ground and building it out with you. All of it runs on the same principle: earn trust before you ask for anything.

2. **The service set** — each as a bordered card with a short "what it is / what you get" split:
   - **Community strategy.** *What it is:* a map of where your buyers gather and how to enter each room. *What you get:* a prioritized plan — communities, angles, norms, and a 90-day play. Often the first engagement.
   - **Reddit growth** `FLAGSHIP`. *What it is:* our deepest specialty — earning durable visibility in the subreddits that matter. *What you get:* standing, presence, and receipts. → full detail on `/reddit`.
   - **Content strategy.** *What it is:* the ideas, formats, and cadence that earn attention in public. *What you get:* a content engine built for communities, not just your blog.
   - **Subreddit creation & management.** *What it is:* build and run a community you own. *What you get:* a launched, moderated, growing room — set up right from day one. *(rate-card line item)*
   - **Embedded / fractional CMO.** *What it is:* Barrie runs marketing as an operator inside your team. *What you get:* senior marketing leadership without a full-time hire — for the right fit. *(secondary — see `00`; keep understated)*

3. **How engagements work** — brief, and deliberately without numbers. Most start with strategy and grow into ongoing work. Frame it as: "Every company's communities are different, so we scope each engagement together — starting with where you are and where your buyers actually gather." **No pricing, rates, tiers, sprint lengths, or retainer figures anywhere on the site.** Pricing is a conversation, not a page.

4. **CTA band** → `/start`.

## /approach — How we work

**Purpose:** De-risk hiring Kind Stranger. Make the method legible and the principles non-negotiable.

**Sections:**

1. **Eyebrow** `THE METHOD` · **H1:** Earn first. Everything else follows.
   **Intro:** Community marketing goes wrong in predictable ways — usually because someone tried to shortcut trust. Our whole method is built to not do that.

2. **The principles** (expanded from the homepage tenets):
   - **Earn first, ask later** — standing before selling.
   - **No astroturf, ever** — the specifics of what we refuse to do, and why it backfires anyway.
   - **Respect the room** — norms first, always.
   - **Say true things** — we'd rather be useful and honest than polished and empty.
   - **Measure what's real** — what we can attribute, what we can't, and how we report it.

3. **How an engagement runs** — a simple phased view: *Listen & map → Earn standing → Scale presence → Report & compound.* Keep it concrete, not a consulting funnel diagram. Describe the phases qualitatively — **no durations, no pricing.**

4. **What you get from us** — a real operator, weekly contact, receipts, and the truth. "You'll get me, not a form-letter and a junior."

5. **CTA** → `/start`.
