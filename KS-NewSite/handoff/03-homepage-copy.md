## 6. The method (the "kind stranger" ethos)

**Eyebrow:** `THE METHOD`

**Headline:** We're practitioners, not posters.

**Intro:** The name isn't decoration. It's how we operate: show up as the generous stranger who adds something, never the one who crashes the party to sell.

**Three tenets (small cards or numbered rows):**
1. **Earn first, ask later.** We build standing in a community before a client is ever mentioned.
2. **No astroturf, ever.** No fake accounts, no bought upvotes, no drive-by pitches. It doesn't work and it's not us.
3. **Respect the room.** Every community has norms. We learn them before we say a word.

**Link:** Our full approach → `/approach`

**Eyebrow:** `THE LONG GAME`

**Headline:** Useful in public pays off twice.

**Body:** When you genuinely help people in the open, it doesn't disappear when the thread scrolls by. The internet increasingly answers questions by repeating what communities already said — so the trust you earn today keeps getting handed to the next person asking, long after you earned it.

## 8. Who it's for (candor block)

**Eyebrow:** `IS THIS YOU?`

**Two columns, honest:**

**A good fit if —** the people you care about gather in communities (a company, a nonprofit, a brand, a public figure — all count); you're playing a long game, not chasing a one-week spike; you'd rather earn trust than rent attention.

**Probably not if —** you want overnight numbers, you're hoping we'll quietly astroturf, or you're not ready to say anything genuinely useful in public.

*(This block is the birthday site's candor — "Is Pura Vida good? Not really." — translated to B2B. Don't soften it into mush; the honesty is the point.)*
